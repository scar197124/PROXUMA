About PROXUMA

Security should make people feel more informed, not more confused.

PROXUMA was created to help people inspect suspicious links, messages, and QR destinations with tools that are clearer, more private, and easier to understand. The goal is not to replace professional security systems. It is to give everyday users a better first line of judgment before they click, trust, or share.

Explore the products

Why PROXUMA exists

Suspicious content often arrives with pressure, urgency, or convincing branding. Most people are expected to make a decision quickly without being shown what actually looks wrong. PROXUMA focuses on slowing that moment down and making the visible evidence easier to understand.

Privacy-first by default

Wherever possible, the tools analyze content locally in the browser. Online checks should remain optional and deliberate rather than hidden in the background.

Explainable results

A score alone is not enough. PROXUMA aims to show what was observed, what remains unverified, and what action makes sense next.

Clear product roles

Each tool has a focused job so users can choose speed, explanation, protection, or investigation without unnecessary complexity.

User-controlled decisions

PROXUMA provides guidance, not automatic authority. The person using the tool stays in control of what is opened, saved, exported, or checked online.

One ecosystem, four distinct lanes

The family is designed as a progression rather than four copies of the same product.

Lite scans quicklyFast local checks for suspicious links.
Sense explainsClearer interpretation of signals and patterns.
Shield protectsDeeper local review, QR intelligence, and reporting.
IT investigatesEvidence-focused link and message intelligence.

What PROXUMA does not claim

  • No local scanner can prove absolute safety. A low-risk result means fewer visible warning signs, not a guarantee.
  • External ownership and reputation may remain unverified. Those checks require separate online services and should be clearly disclosed.
  • PROXUMA is an aid for judgment. It does not replace antivirus software, enterprise monitoring, law enforcement, or professional incident response.
  • Privacy boundaries matter. Any future online feature should explain what leaves the device and require a deliberate user action.
